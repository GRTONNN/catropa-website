Cat Care Essentials for a Happy, Healthy Crew üíï

Whether you're welcoming a new whiskered friend or deepening your bond with a longtime companion, these tips help keep your feline family thriving.

Feeding & Nutrition
  • Feed adults twice daily; kittens need smaller, more frequent meals.
  • Choose high-protein, meat-first foods. Cats are obligate carnivores.
  • Keep fresh water available at all times. A fountain can make hydration more fun.
  • Adjust diets for age, allergies, or health needs‚Äîyour vet can guide you.
  • Never share foods like onions, garlic, chocolate, or alcohol. They're toxic to cats.
Grooming & Hygiene
  • Brush long-haired cats daily; short-haired ones weekly.
  • Bathe only when needed‚Äîcats are excellent self-groomers.
  • Trim nails every few weeks to prevent snags and scratches.
  • Clean ears gently and brush teeth regularly (or schedule vet cleanings).
Health & Vet Care
  • Schedule annual checkups for vaccines and wellness exams.
  • Spay or neuter to support long-term health and reduce behavioral issues.
  • Watch for changes in appetite, litter box habits, or energy levels.
  • Signs of wellness include a shiny coat, clear eyes, and playful curiosity.
Environment & Enrichment
  • Scoop litter daily and place boxes in quiet, low-traffic areas.
  • Offer cozy beds, blankets, and hideaways for comfort and security.
  • Rotate toys, add puzzle feeders, and encourage climbing or scratching.
  • Secure windows and balconies to keep curious explorers safe.
Behavior & Socialization
  • Respect their boundaries. Tail flicks, ear positions, and meows say a lot.
  • Introduce new pets or guests slowly to reduce stress.
  • Stick to routines. Predictable feeding, playtime, and cuddles build trust.
