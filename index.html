    The Queen’s Second Chance.

    Once upon a whisker, in a world steeped in the aroma of karak tea, sweet treats, and the warm bustle of Arab hospitality, there lived a regal Persian beauty named Juliet. To those closest to her, she was simply Bebi. With her silken coat of light gray and white, and eyes as green and mesmerizing as an oasis at dusk, Bebi reigned supreme, not over palaces or gardens, but over the villa kingdom, where she was the undisputed queen.

    But Juliet wasn’t just a pretty face. She had a love story worthy of Shakespeare. Her partner? A dashing feline named Romeo. Together, they shared cozy naps, synchronized tail flicks, and even raised a litter of adorable kittens. Life was purrfect.

    Then came a twist in the tale. Juliet’s human, her original Furr Parent, received surprising news: a baby on the way, and the doctor’s orders to avoid cats. It was a heartbreaking moment. But instead of turning their backs on Juliet, her humans did something beautiful; they chose love over abandonment. They offered her up for adoption, hoping someone would see the magic in her.

    And that’s when fate stepped in.

    On May 21, 2014. Juliet found her new chapter with a kind-hearted officemate (that’s us!). From the moment she stepped into her new home, she brought elegance, sass, and a whole lot of cuddles. Her journey to become a beloved family member is a reminder that every cat deserves a second chance and a soft place to land.

Did You Know?

  • Cats sleep for about 70% of their lives.
  • A group of cats is called a clowder.
  • Cats can make over 100 different sounds.

Fun Facts

  • Cats have five toes on their front paws but only four on their back paws.
  • The oldest known pet cat was found in a 9,500-year-old grave in Cyprus.
  • Cats can rotate their ears 180 degrees.
  • The oldest cat lived 38 years.
  • Cats have 32 muscles in each ear.
  • The Maine Coon is the world’s largest domestic cat breed.

A Brief History of Cats

Cats were domesticated around 9,000 years ago in the Near East. They were highly valued in Ancient Egypt, often associated with gods and royalty.

Origins of Domestic Cats

The domestic cat is believed to have descended from the African wildcat. Over centuries, they spread across the globe through human trade and migration.
