Welcome to CATropa!

A celebration of our eight rescued cats — each with their own charm, quirks, and heart-melting tales.

From hilarious habits to heroic rescues, you’ll meet the fabulous feline stars of CATropa Crew — whiskered wonders with unforgettable personalities. This isn’t just a showcase, it’s a reminder: every cat deserves love, care, and a forever home. 🐾

Meet the Crew

🐾 Juliet

The Queen’s Second Chance

Nickname(s): Bebi, Julieta Magalpok

Breed: Persian

Birthday: May 21, 2014

Age: 12 years old

Eye Color: Green

Fur Color: Solid Light Gray & White

Pattern: Bi-color

Weight: 2.5–3 kg

Adoption / Rescue Tale

Once upon a whisker, in a world steeped in the aroma of karak tea, sweet treats, and the warm bustle of Arab hospitality, there lived a regal Persian beauty named Juliet. To those closest to her, she was simply Bebi. With her silken coat of light gray and white, and eyes as green and mesmerizing as an oasis at dusk, Bebi reigned supreme, not over palaces or gardens, but over the villa kingdom, where she was the undisputed queen.

But Juliet wasn’t only a pretty face. She had a love story worthy of Shakespeare. Her partner? A dashing feline named Romeo. Together, they shared cozy naps, synchronized tail flicks, and even raised a litter of adorable kittens. Life was purrfect.

Then came a twist. Juliet’s human, her original Furr Parent, received surprising news: a baby on the way, and the doctor’s orders to avoid cats. It was a heartbreaking moment. But instead of turning their backs on Juliet, her humans did something beautiful, they chose love over abandonment. They offered her up for adoption, hoping someone would see the magic in her.

And that’s when fate stepped in.

On May 21, 2014, Juliet found her new chapter with a kind-hearted officemate (that’s us!). From the moment she stepped into her new home, she brought elegance, sass, and a whole lot of cuddles. Her journey to become a beloved family member is a reminder that every cat deserves a second chance and a soft place to land.

Paws for Hope

Not broken, paused, waiting for the right heart to notice.

Personality & Purr-sonal Life

Personality Vibes: Elegant, calm, carries herself like royalty. Also surprises with playful Kung Fu moves.

Quirks & Habits: Loves jumping to high places, sometimes falls but pretends it was planned. Prefers fresh, clean water bowls.

Favorite Activities: Sleeping in dramatic poses.

Best Friends: Romeo and Mikmik.

Adoption / Rescue Tale

Mikmik’s tale began in Ajman, where she grew up alongside her former owner’s child. She was once the sunshine of the household, playful, curious, and always ready to pounce into fun. But one day, her spark faded. She grew quiet, curling up in corners instead of chasing shadows. Her owner, sadly, lost interest and decided it was time to let her go.

The moment we met her, she looked straight at me and let out the softest, most soulful mikyaw. Our friend was stunned. “She never meows,” he said. “She never even moves unless she feels safe.” Right then, I knew, we were meant to be her forever family.

After bringing her to the vet, we discovered she had ear mites and an upset tummy. At first, we thought we’d help her recover and then find her a loving home. But twice she was offered, and twice she was gently turned away. Each time, she came back to us, quietly, patiently, as if she already knew…

So we listened with open hearts.

We cuddled through quiet moments.

We loved her, just as she was.

And decided to keep her fur-ever ♥

Paws for Hope

Faded spark, quiet heart, just waiting to be seen.

Quirks & Habits: Her signature mikyaw is rare and precious, reserved for moments of true connection. She has a heart-melting habit of placing her paw gently on your face when you’re sad, especially with children. It’s her way of saying, “I’m here.”

Favorite Activities:

Adoption / Rescue Tale

Way back in 2018, we first met Maku at the pantry door of an office in Al Montazah Park. She was tiny, barely a month old, thin, hungry, and heartbreakingly wounded. Her left arm had a deep, visible injury, and you could feel the pain she was carrying just by looking at her.

The next day, we returned with food for the stray kittens. Maku ate gratefully, and that’s when we knew, we couldn’t leave her behind. We brought her to the vet, where she was gently sedated so her infected wound could be thoroughly cleaned. Her cries during treatment were raw and real, proof of the pain she had endured far too early in life.

The vet told us plainly, if we hadn’t brought her in, she would’ve slowly died from the infection. At first, we planned to foster her only until she healed. But the universe had other plans. Seven years later, she’s still with us, strong, sassy, and proudly living her Alpha attitude. She may have started life in pain, but today she rules her world with confidence, courage, and a whole lot of cattitude.

Tiny, wounded, and fighting for a chance to be seen.

As the proud Alpha of the crew, she’s protective, independent, and always one paw ahead. But beneath that bold exterior is a survivor’s heart and a soft spot for those she loves.

Adoption / Rescue Tale

Nika was a familiar face in our neighborhood, a quiet, scruffy little cat who lived near the entrance of our residential building. She wasn’t loud or demanding, only patiently waiting each day for someone kind enough to offer her a meal. We’d often see her sitting by the doorway, watching people come and go like a silent gatekeeper.

Though we fed her regularly, Nika remained aloof and wary of humans. We never knew why, maybe life hadn’t been kind to her before. But day by day, she started to recognize us. She’d stare as we left for work and wait for us to return, her eyes following us like she was trying to understand our rhythm.

What truly tugged at our hearts was her uncanny resemblance to our beloved cat back in the Philippines, Niko. It felt like fate had placed her there, waiting for us to notice. And one day, we did more than notice—we brought her home. Her first act as a newly adopted cat? She pooped right at our doorstep. 😂 A nervous welcome, but one we’ll never forget.

Now at six years old, Nika is still a bit skittish. Trips to the vet or unfamiliar sounds can trigger her “panic poop” or “fear pee” reflex. But she’s ours, quirks and all—and we wouldn’t trade her for the world.

Adoption / Rescue Tale

Just across from our home is Sharjah Coop, where we buy our groceries. That’s where we first met her, a fierce little tortoiseshell cat who ruled the entrance like a queen. She was the only cat living there, she didn’t tolerate intruders. Any stray who dared step into her territory was swiftly chased off. But beneath that tough exterior, she had a soft spot for humans. She loved people more than fellow furry creatures. Every night after work, we’d feed her along with the other strays. She never begged or made noise, just waited patiently, always calm, always present. Then one day, she was gone. Days passed. Then weeks. No sign of her. We hoped someone with a kind heart had adopted her. But the truth was far more heartbreaking.

She returned, wounded, limping, with a curved shoulder and a deep, long gash down her back. We didn’t know what had happened, but we knew one thing: she needed help. We didn’t hesitate. We took her in, found a vet, and started raising funds to cover her treatment. It was the height of COVID, and vet care in the UAE is expensive. But thanks to generous souls who stepped in, she got the care she needed.

Fast forward: we adopted her without a second thought. After all the pain, the struggle, and the near-death experience she endured, all we wanted was to give her a better life, a safe, loving home where she could finally rest. Today, she’s healed. We hope her heart and mind are healing, too. She shows us love every day, grateful in ways only a rescued soul can be. We named her Kali, short for Kalayaan, because we rescued her on June 12, 2020, Philippine Independence Day. She’s our little warrior, our symbol of freedom, and our reminder that love always finds a way.

Adoption / Rescue Tale

Down by the sand parking near our building, Happy Home, Sharjah, lived a tiny black-and-white cat we called Max Ruru. Two-toned, wide-eyed, and always alone. While other strays chased each other or begged for food, Max spent his days quietly playing with dry leaves and bits of plastic like they were prized possessions. He had a whole playground to himself… but no playmates. We used to wonder: "Why doesn’t anyone want to play with Max? He wasn’t aggressive. He wasn’t loud. He was just… different. One day, a friend of ours was looking to adopt a cat. We immediately thought of Max. He was cute, handsome, and clearly in need of companionship. So we scooped him up and gave him his first taste of a furrever home. Everything was perfect, until it wasn’t. Turns out, both of our friends’ daughters had cat allergies. Heartbroken, they had to return Max. And suddenly, we were faced with a dilemma. We already had six cats, Juliet, Mikmik, Maku, Nika, Mermer, and Potpot, and we weren’t sure we could handle one more. We worried about space, care, medication, and whether we could give him the love he truly deserved.

But Max had already chosen us. And honestly? We couldn’t say no. Fast forward four years: that tiny leaf-playing boy is now a full-grown gentleman, still a little misunderstood, but deeply loved. We’ve noticed something special about him. Other cats tend to avoid him, and we’re not entirely sure why. The vet suspects he may have special needs, or maybe it’s just his white fur that makes him seem like a threat to the others. Whatever the reason, Max has always been a bit of an outsider in the CATropa crew. But to us? He’s soft as cotton, warm as a blanket, and full of quiet love that only we truly understand.

His mortal enemies? Nika and Maku. There’s tension. There’s drama. There’s the occasional side-eye across the room. But we’re holding out hope that one day, a miracle will happen, and they’ll all share a sunbeam in peace.

Adoption / Rescue Tale

At first, we thought he was just another stray asking for food, like so many others who find their way to us. We met him near our home, at the ADNOC petrol station, where we regularly fed the local crew of street cats. He approached quietly, no meows, no fuss. Just a slow walk and a tired stare. But then we saw it. His legs were wrapped in a makeshift bandage, and there was a deep, raw cut on his thigh. This wasn’t a cat asking for food, this was a cat asking for help. We rushed him to the vet. The doctor believed he’d been run over, treated briefly, and then dumped back on the street to “heal” on his own. No one knew how long he’d been like that, days, maybe weeks. His wound was badly infected, full of maggots, and so deep you could almost see the bone.

We didn’t hesitate. We took him in, determined to help him heal. But it wasn’t easy. The CATropa crew didn’t welcome him. He was white, with patches of black and yellow, and somehow they saw him as a threat. Maybe it was his scent, his energy, maybe just the trauma he carried. His wound refused to heal quickly. Alongside antibiotics, we used Manuka honey, a natural remedy with healing powers, to help close the wound. It took over a year before it fully healed. And another year before he could walk and play properly again. But through it all, one cat stood by him: Max Ruru. Max, our quiet leaf collector, saw something in Sensei. Maybe it was the loneliness. Maybe it was the pain. Maybe it was just fate. Max loved him like a best friend. And honestly? Max convinced us to keep him. He needed a companion, and Sensei had already stolen our hearts.

We named him Sensei because he taught us patience, resilience, and the quiet strength of survival. Oh, and the best part? He has a marking on his body shaped like Mickey Mouse. When we first saw him, we joked that he looked like a mouse, tiny, fragile, and oddly adorable. But now? He’s soft, kind, and silky smooth. A survivor with a shiny coat and a heart full of gratitude. Sensei didn’t just heal. He became family.

Pet Care Tips for a Happy, Healthy Crew 💕

Whether you're welcoming a new whiskered friend or deepening your bond with a longtime companion, these tips help keep your feline family thriving.

Feeding & Nutrition
  • Feed adults twice daily; kittens need smaller, more frequent meals.
  • Choose high-protein, meat-first foods. Cats are obligate carnivores.
  • Keep fresh water available at all times. A fountain can make hydration more fun.
  • Adjust diets for age, allergies, or health needs—your vet can guide you.
  • Never share foods like onions, garlic, chocolate, or alcohol. They're toxic to cats.
Grooming & Hygiene
  • Brush long-haired cats daily; short-haired ones weekly.
  • Bathe only when needed—cats are excellent self-groomers.
  • Trim nails every few weeks to prevent snags and scratches.
  • Clean ears gently and brush teeth regularly (or schedule vet cleanings).
Health & Vet Care
  • Schedule annual checkups for vaccines and wellness exams.
  • Spay or neuter to support long-term health and reduce behavioral issues.
  • Watch for changes in appetite, litter box habits, or energy levels.
  • Signs of wellness include a shiny coat, clear eyes, and playful curiosity.
Environment & Enrichment
  • Scoop litter daily and place boxes in quiet, low-traffic areas.
  • Offer cozy beds, blankets, and hideaways for comfort and security.
  • Rotate toys, add puzzle feeders, and encourage climbing or scratching.
  • Secure windows and balconies to keep curious explorers safe.
Behavior & Socialization
  • Respect their boundaries. Tail flicks, ear positions, and meows say a lot.
  • Introduce new pets or guests slowly to reduce stress.
  • Stick to routines. Predictable feeding, playtime, and cuddles build trust.

Did You Know?

From ancient feline legends to quirky biological facts, cats have a way of surprising us. This section celebrates the curious, the clever, and the downright delightful truths about our whiskered companions.

Explore fun facts about cat behavior, history, and their unique place in human culture.

The Art of Rest & Awareness
  • Cats sleep for about 70% of their lives.
  • Cats can make over 100 different sounds.
  • Cats have 32 muscles in each ear.
  • Cats can rotate their ears 180 degrees.
Anatomy of Meow
  • Cats have five toes on their front paws but only four on their back paws.
  • Each whisker is rooted deep in the skin and connected to a sensory receptor—cats use them to navigate tight spaces.
  • Their paw pads act like shock absorbers and help them move silently, making them stealthy hunters.
  • Cats walk on their toes—a trait called digitigrade locomotion—which gives them their graceful, tiptoe stride.
The Meowseum
  • The oldest known pet cat was found in a 9,500-year-old grave in Cyprus.
  • The oldest cat lived 38 years.
  • The Maine Coon is the world’s largest domestic cat breed.
